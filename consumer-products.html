The new frontier of efficiency is about sustainability

While waste reduction continues to be a top focus for continuous improvement, further gains to efficiency require the addition of sensors to capture micro-losses as well as digitization of paper-based processes to reduce information-related waste.

Losses are captured as part of Overall Equipment Efficiency (or Overall Labor Efficiency), but this index also needs to include losses tied to sustainability metrics such as water, air, gas, electricity, and steam, which drive cost efficiency and brand equity.

Attract and retain a quality workforce to improve productivity

Faced with a scarcity of skilled workers, manufacturers are responding by boosting recruitment, adopting new technologies and taking steps to maximize the productivity and experience of their older workers.

Learning by doing is the most effective method for improving knowledge and skills. By digitizing paper-based processes, workflow management enables step-by-step guidance to improve productivity, ensure compliance and minimize human error.
